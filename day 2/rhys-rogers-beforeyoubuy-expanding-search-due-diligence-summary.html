Talk summary — voice transcription (ASR cleaned)

Rhys Rogers (Beforeyoubuy). For audiences outside Australia: explains building/pest and strata reports, cashback model, marketplace of inspectors, and why portals should own the move from search into due diligence. References Stephen (Snow), Patrick / CoreLogic from prior PPW sessions. Source: voice memo — numbers and names as stated; not audited.

Why explain the product

Non-Australian delegates may not know the category: when a buyer is serious, they need building and pest and strata reports — sold at volume nationally. The talk connects that purchase journey to portals and trust.

Cashback / shared-cost story

Illustrative trio (Simon, Rhys, Scott) on the same SA property: each might pay on the order of ~$700 for their own report — wasteful overlap. Beforeyoubuy introduces a cashback / discount model on portal channels (e.g. REA, Domain): same report class at a lower out-of-pocket (example narrative: $400 vs $700, $300 back), so the buyer who participates effectively subsidizes or recoups versus peers who pay full freight. Goal: lower true cost, better experience, and alignment with listing sites buyers already use.

Product learning: some users wanted pay upfront options; model evolved for different markets that can mirror the same mechanics.

From inquiry → trust → transaction

Strategic shift: not only lead capture but trust and a real transactional relationship in the DD phase.

Founder lens: M&A vs real estate transparency

Prior career in restructuring / insolvency (e.g. with Deloitte) — hundreds of companies, dozens of deals — where due diligence in the data room was exhaustive; hard questions created tension and surfaced risk. Residential real estate historically did the opposite — limited transparency. That contrast motivated building something that moves the industry toward disclosure buyers actually want.

“Everyone wants transparency”

Buyers want to understand schools, defects, and whether someone is overselling “the best.” Light humour about slides being on the record for his kids in 30 years — underscoring that the product thesis is long-term defensibility through real value.

Six buckets & marketplace

Product scope sketched across strata, building/pest, and adjacent services (e.g. utilities, insurance, data). ~500 inspectors on the marketplace — not employed; BYB supplies technology and demand. Agents are not the inspectors; the platform orchestrates the ecosystem.

Value by stakeholder (spoken)

Vendors / campaigns: Easier to get reports in the cooling-off / pre-auction window, keep the deal moving. Buyers: Same benefits plus download / access to structured outputs. Agents: Every report purchase yields identity signals — name, phone, email, investor vs first-home buyer, etc. Agents call this the best email they get: among 20 open-home visitors, the subset who bought a report are the highest-intent calls (card committed, serious about the asset). Cross-sell: e.g. discount on an inspection for the buyer’s own property when they download a report — more touchpoints for the agent.

Reference to Patrick / CoreLogic-style theme: quality data back to the agent for workflow and listing intelligence.

Scale metrics (from talk)

Order-of-magnitude claims: ~6,000 agents in the last 12 months; ~126,000 reports sold; footprint described as ~2% of Australian listings or ~23% of inspection/reports market (verify wording in recording); ~200,000 “super hot” buyer/seller touches; growth year on year.

Data, templates, and AI

Strata: Own stack (e.g. Strata Force-style product) feeding structured data, especially NSW through their app. Building/pest: Many inspector templates — AI / parsing used to normalize into a consistent structured format for analytics and UX.

Why not only ChatGPT on a PDF

Users dump reports into ChatGPT and are underwhelmed: no comparative baseline. With a large corpus, BYB can answer relative questions — e.g. how common is rising damp in this segment vs 23% of reports in the area — so the buyer gets context, not a one-off summary. Ties back to conversational due diligence slide.

Google SEM vs portal-native DD

Billions still flow to Google; portals can offer a more seamless path from search → transaction if they embed DD. Emotional reality: Australian buyers still engage multiple agents — opportunity to convert search intent into trusted DD and revenue on-portal.

Economics

BYB’s margin profile cited as high (order of ~70%) vs other bracket businesses at 20–30%. Inspections market in Australia described as $300M+. Portals spending to compete on leads could reallocate toward DD / composition that protects core listing revenue.

Closing argument

Portals moving into DD is not trivial — but the win is a system where portals and members succeed, core offerings are protected, and new revenue grows. (Aligns with ecosystem slide footer.)

Audience Q&A (ASR cleaned)

End of session; moderator / audience names partly garbled in ASR (e.g. Brian, Sharon).

Could this work in Israel (and other markets)?

Yes in principle where the same report + DD dynamics exist. Rhys affirms Australia as home market. Light humour about UK calls, toddlers, and four-day-week expectations — hard to run UK hours from AU.

Getting buyers to purchase DD before they go deeper in the journey?

If you can surface report purchase early — before they exhaust the rest of the path — you get further down-funnel value and stronger attachment to your stack (“once you get that far, you’re going to sell the rest” — paraphrase).

Biggest pushback from agents? Interest in more exhaustive checks?

Pushback is rarely “it’s not worth paying for.” It’s time-to-adoption: a long track to get live, educate offices, and wire day-to-day handling of leads and reports. Success looks like giving agents the tools to operationalize — not reorganizing “six groups.” Contrast with large portals (REA-class) where product and distribution are already built for scale; BYB positions its own lighter-weight / “micro” surfaces (e.g. address-level cues on whether a report is available on the listing) that plug into that world.

What’s the biggest “hit” for you right now?

AI tooling speed. Story arc: weeks ago still clumsy at testing; within days, rapid research / build cycles; ~10 days later shipping closable slices of product that weren’t imaginable before — using instruction-driven dev assistants (ASR: “chord” → likely Cursor or similar). Ideas → prototypes in days; as of ~two weeks before the talk, pace had step-changed.

Will you expand into mandatory energy-efficiency ratings / upgrades (NABERS, EPC-style, sqm rules)?

Depends on government and market structure in Australia. Hard to rely on four or five small providers to carry a national ratings mandate; likely consolidation or designated players. BYB will follow where policy and demand settle — detail in recording partly garbled (e.g. “Chinese are zero” may be carbon / NABERS shorthand — verify).

Closing

Thanks for the exchange; BYB sits in the broader proptech stack; building these systems is years-long work (order of ~11 years cited — confirm). Appreciation to moderator/guests.
