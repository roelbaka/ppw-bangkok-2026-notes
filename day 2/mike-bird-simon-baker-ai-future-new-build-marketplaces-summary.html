AI and the Future of New Build Marketplaces

Fireside / interview — new-build and off-the-plan in Australia: data, voice AI, call intelligence, and how developer marketing sits next to dominant listing portals.

Market structure: Australia and new build

Australia’s established residential market is described as dominated by two large players (“gorillas”). The new development / off-the-plan category is framed as under-served relative to typical resale search — different buyer journey, heavier finance and complexity (e.g. long forward commitments on deposits / construction timelines, on the order of ~40 months in the local context). Selling a large tower vs many boutique projects implies different lead volumes and go-to-market needs.

Origin story and focus

The business traces back roughly 15 years to digital tooling aimed at property developers, with evolution through adjacent publishing / media assets and consolidation toward the developer segment as the strategic center. The thesis: own the problems developers care about (demand, trust, conversion) rather than only replicating generic listing mechanics.

Developers as marketers; buyer mix

Developers are treated as marketers of their own inventory. Buyer composition skews heavily toward investors and second-home buyers in their data (order of ~85% cited as “value investing” / non-primary-occupier framing in the discussion). Primary-residence emotional buying exists but is not the core volume story for this channel.

Channels and “AI voice”

Go-to-market spans website, email, reviews, social, and an AI voice agent positioned as deeply informed on developments nationally — a way to scale first-touch exploration without every enquiry hitting a human immediately. The stack is described as combining technology with literature / content and aggregation of data to support buyers and internal teams.

Subscription model

Revenue is primarily subscription-based (not “sell yourself” as a one-off listing fee narrative). Trust-building with developers matters; delivery includes social, video, and ongoing engagement — topic of conversation extended to AI and the future of the built environment.

Call data, transcripts, and economics

A major lever is historical call volume at scale (example scale: thousands of calls per week over years; large projects such as a 300-apartment tower in a major CBD). Transcripts feed models (discussion referenced an “Omaha”-style or similar approach to call transcript analysis — wording unclear in ASR). Insight goals: which areas buyers ask about, demand for pipeline projects, and how to re-use conversational data for marketing and product.

Economic shift: Running many calls through a first-line human team became hard to justify; a large share of volume is lower-intent. Direction of travel: AI-first handling for initial qualification, with humans on higher-value steps — potentially fewer agents but better paid / more productive, and more budget for performance media.

Data assets vs portals and “ChatGPT search”

Positioning vs portals: portals have enormous distribution; this model stresses proprietary behavioral and conversational data outside the main platforms. On AI search: skepticism that a raw ChatGPT-style property search “wins” alone; more plausible that incumbent brands retain mindshare and integrate AI as copilot inside existing search — harder for a standalone generic model to own the whole journey.

Acquisitions and execution

Acquisitions are largely set aside in favor of speed and internal alignment: leadership, product, and go-to-market moving together. Large integrations are framed as slow and distracting relative to shipping with a smaller team.

Geography

References to Melbourne, national coverage, and scale across Australian developers (e.g. ~25% of “resolution” / relevant developers in market cited as clients — verify wording in recording).
