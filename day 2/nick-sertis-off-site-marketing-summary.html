Off Site Marketing – Does it Make a Difference?

Global ad trends, passive vs active discovery, and automated multi-channel activation for portals and marketplaces — with performance and attribution back into the stack.

Nick Sertis

Property Portal Watch Conference · The next wave of digital real estate · Online Marketplaces

Session

Nick Sertis presents the case for off-site marketing: buyers spend most of their time not on the marketplace, so inventory must be visible across social, search, video, and messaging — with automation and AI-assisted optimization to keep cost and complexity manageable. Talk summary — voice transcription (ASR cleaned)

Figures below are as stated in the talk, not fact-checked.

Scale of digital advertising

Global digital advertising is described as having grown very large (order of hundreds of billions USD; speaker cited movement from roughly ~$140B toward ~$700B+ over a multi-year window and expected 2025 figures around ~$770B globally and a major regional bucket around ~$172B — treat as directional). The punchline for portals: budgets and attention are shifting; on-site alone misses most of the journey.

Discovery is social and “passive” first

Audience engagement has moved toward social discovery, short-form video, and search suggestions before a user ever opens the portal. Marketing must assume passive exposure (scrolling feeds) as well as active research (intent-heavy search on site). The two modes need different creative and targeting logic.

Consideration window: six to ten weeks (example)

A stylized funnel: initial passive impression → a consideration phase (~6–10 weeks in the example) where the same user moves into active search and comparison → decision phase with concrete shortlisting and booking/viewing intent. By the time someone is deep on the marketplace, much of the story is already shaped off-site.

“70%” before the marketplace deep-dive

Speaker argues a large share of the decision (order of ~70% in the narrative) happens before heavy on-platform behavior — so off-site presence is not optional if you want to influence consideration.

Evidence: NAR-style survey on lead quality

Reference to a National Association of Realtors-style survey: respondents ranked channels that drive high-quality leads; social media scored highest for a large minority (order of ~39% of responses in the telling), with CRM, portals, and other channels trailing in the chart described.

Time on site vs “other” hours

Contrast between long passive hours on social/video vs short active sessions on the marketplace when in “buying mode.” Implication: retargeting and always-on off-site campaigns must cover the long tail, not only burst activity during on-site sessions.

Automation and cross-channel

Manual multi-channel management hits scale, consistency, and cost limits. Automation enables: fast activation from listing feeds, budget fluidity between channels based on performance, consistent brand and templates, and operation without huge in-house media teams. Channels mentioned include social, Google search, video, and integrations such as WhatsApp.

Product architecture (high level)

Described as an API-led layer: integrate listings, push through an activation / orchestration engine, generate campaign variants (creative and targeting), with AI reviewing performance statistics to improve outcomes. Attribution and reporting feed back to the portal’s own systems / CRM.

Commercial outcomes (claims)

Examples cited in session (verify in deck): strong lead performance uplift for participating inventory vs non-participating; in one European case, networked advertising associated with higher achieved prices and faster sales vs control — framed as case evidence, not guarantees.

Audience Q&A (from memo)

AI and passive discovery?

Yes — AI helps interpret signals across passive touchpoints and tune what gets shown; complements the funnel story above.

Lead quality vs volume?

Product can emphasize qualification in some markets; trade-off between stricter filters (fewer but “hotter” leads) vs broader capture. Some clients want borderline leads for nurture; others want strict pre-qual. Positioning is configurable.
