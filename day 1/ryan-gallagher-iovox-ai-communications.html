Three pillars (voice memo + deck)

Capture SMS, phone, email, chat — all channels pulled into one data layer.
Analyse Massive scale: 1B+ phone calls in their universe; models trained on that data.
Automate Growing part of the business: fix problems found in analysis — AI agents, workflows, callbacks.
“AI is easy, data is hard” Gallagher: that line is music to iovox’s ears — they are fundamentally a data company using it to analyse businesses continuously.

How they analyse

  • Own models trained on iovox data; PII-specific models (generic PII tooling often not accurate enough).
  • Multilingual.
  • Micro data: What happens in one call — who answered, was it professional, was a booking made (viewing, broker, etc.)? → Real-time triggers (e.g. mortgage mention → inject CRM; no answer → message + schedule callback — some clients want same day, others next-day report).
  • Macro data: Trends across hundreds of thousands of calls — answer rate, booking rate, sentiment up/down — then act on it.

How calls are captured (three levels)

  • Metadata (15+ years): Who, when, how many calls, length, answered or not — no recording required.
  • Inference from audio (no recording): Increasingly important where markets are reluctant to record (privacy); detect baselines, spikes, patterns from the waveform.
  • Recorded + transcribed: Deepest signal; confirms what actually happened; also trains the non-recorded models.

Real estate — aggregate benchmarks (iovox data)

Snapshot across many customers (aggregate, not one client).

  • Sentiment: Only 18.8% of callers end up “happy” — bad for agency and portal brand (people blame “the portal” when the agency doesn’t answer).
  • After-hours: Only ~3% of calls — but ~50% of those within one hour of closing; extra staff hour could capture a big share.
  • Answer rate: 47% of calls answered by a live agent — over half get no live pick-up; during business hours behaviour still poor.
  • Callbacks: ~5% of callers complain nobody called back (significant share of those who do get through).
  • Data capture: Agents ask for name/email/phone on ~87% of calls — but that doesn’t mean it lands in CRM; 90%+ of people who call may still be unknown for follow-up.
  • Viewings booked on the call: 7.1%.
  • Mortgage discussed: 6.1% average; one agency claimed 100% staff ask — iovox measured 13% for them.
  • “Do you have a property to sell?” (dual-mandate gold) — <1% of agents ask.

Where leads vanish

  • 12% hang up (hold too long, hate IVR, etc.) — never reach voicemail.
  • 41% hit voicemail — but only ~9% leave a message (don’t trust voicemail / don’t expect callback).
  • → ~44% “lost lead” visibility — huge spend to drive calls, then no usable identity or intent.

Branch benchmarking

Same brand: branch answer rates from 21% to 75%. Some operators with 100+ branches losing money for years — iovox often finds nobody’s answering and/or unprofessional handling when they do. Verticals: real estate, automotive, vets, healthcare — branch variance is a recurring lever.

Slide: flow from voicemail to AI and follow-up. Aligns with voice memo: ~41% voicemail, ~9% leave message → AI voicemail can triple messages and recover “invisible” leads.

Voicemail

Using an interactive AI agent for voicemail can triple the number of messages left. Example: 4,100 calls to voicemail, only 9.05% leave a message; iovox can increase that by 3× → +1,810 leads, 923 with intent. Low estimate: $92,300 extra revenue from tackling missed voicemails.

Dumb IVR replacement

An AI agent can take a message, qualify the caller, and direct calls better than a static IVR. Estimate: +600 leads, $60,000 value.

Staff hours

Staff working one extra hour (e.g. with AI handling overflow). +153 leads, $15,300 value.

Solutions (voice memo)

  • Know the problem first — gap analysis; then supplement with AI agents, training, etc.
  • Keyword example: Medical chain underperforming branches — word “Amazon” kept appearing: people saying they can get it on Amazon, don’t need to visit. Actionable insight from comms data.
  • Voicemail → interactive AI agent: People hate classic voicemail; AI agent is friendlier, can give info; ~3× more leave a message → attacks the 44% blind spot. If voicemails checked only weekly, ~80% of time-sensitive bookings/viewings are already dead — speed matters.
  • “Dumb IVR” → conversational AI: Example customer with 11-step press-1-2-3 IVR replaced by “Hi, how can I help?” Some clients use AI to call back; others cherry-pick best leads from IVR overflow to a call centre.
  • Philosophy: Not replacing humans with robots blindly — replace dumb machines with smart ones, filter noise so humans handle high-value and sensitive cases.
  • 24/7 agents (“snappy”): ~50% after-hours calls within an hour of close — agents qualify upfront or catch overflow; hospitality pattern: table booking / FAQ to bot, “real issue” to person so staff answer when it matters.
  • Integrations: Customers may use other big agent platforms — iovox feeds data to see where bots fail (e.g. 30% of “leads” were for services the business doesn’t offer) and iterate.

Q&A (voice memo)

Internal AI at iovox

  • QC, testing, faster dev, documentation, gap analysis (e.g. API usage per function).
  • Train own models on proprietary data; PII: US clients often want who said what; EU wants redaction in transcripts.
  • Message: “Your job won’t be taken by AI — it’ll be taken by someone who uses AI.” Resistance from devs; product now goes from idea to interactive prototype in minutes in meetings instead of static wireframes.

Spam / fraud on comms

  • Models to flag suspicious conversations; SMS to parties “don’t proceed.”
  • AI-generated spam: Not huge yet but coming. Easier to block repetitive patterns (same script many times/minute); harder for smarter attacks. SMS easier to filter pre-human than voice.

Data silos & customers

  • Data is siloed per customer / country / industry — regulations and contracts differ everywhere.
  • Customers want transcripts for training and product — but fear feeding public LLMs; iovox pushes own models and careful handling of sensitive fields.

Humans vs AI

  • ~50% of people OK talking to machines for some tasks — replace stupid automation, not every human touch.
  • Special cases (e.g. big party, complex ask) → human; routine apartment search → bot OK. Filter noise so staff focus on highest value and sensitivity.

One-page takeaway

  • iovox = capture all comms → analyse at micro + macro → automate (AI agents, IVR, voicemail).
  • Real estate phones are broken — sentiment, answer rates, lost voicemail, portal brand damage.
  • Data moat + replace dumb IVR/voicemail; integrate with other agents; prove ROI with numbers on slide.
  • AI internally + externally; spam next wave; humans for high-touch.
