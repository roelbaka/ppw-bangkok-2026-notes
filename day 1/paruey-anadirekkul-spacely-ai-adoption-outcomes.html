Why adoption ≠ outcomes

  • Scale of access: ChatGPT-scale growth — e.g. ~800M users in a short window — more than ~12× Thailand’s population. Free tiers, open weights, $20–25 paid plans → access is commoditised.
  • Same tools, different results (e.g. booking visits): failure in production is often execution — bolt-on AI without changing workflow, policies, or who decides what to do with outputs (images, copy, code).
  • Bottleneck: decision bandwidth — lots of AI output, same old approval paths → activity without outcomes.
Practical map: (1) Where do decisions slow down? (2) Where can you cut alignment friction (data/model clarity to decide)? (3) Measure outcomes — did AI shorten cycles, raise revenue, cut cost? Not only “how many staff use ChatGPT.”

AI becomes “invisible”

  • Customers will expect fast, cheap, high quality — won’t care which model; AI embeds in existing tools (parallel to mobile maturing from separate “mobile team” to infrastructure).
  • Companies may run dedicated AI units; execution-heavy industries (e.g. real estate) benefit most when alignment improves early — design choices drive later cost.

Imagination friction

  • Definition: Hard to visualise the end state → hesitation, slower decisions, less confidence → slower business.
  • Many teams push more data, nudges, promos; another lever is removing blockers: uncertainty, misalignment, imagination friction (like a parking brake — more gas doesn’t help until it’s released).

What Spacely does

  • AI platform for agents, brokers, designers, architects — e.g. empty → furnished / spatial options (“this could be your hall / coffee shop”).
  • UK — Artistics (acoustic interiors): proposal visuals ~one week → ~one hour; faster client turnaround, clearer decisions.
  • Thailand — Bangkok Asset (second-hand / renovation): virtual staging every image; was manual Photoshop, ~days per photo → hours with AI; marketing saw higher engagement, buyers reacting (“cool / interested”), more leads.

Business direction

  • Today: usage-based SaaS; moving toward a platform where visualization / rhetoric-of-space work gets done end-to-end.
  • US: Expansion targeted around November (year per session — e.g. 2026).
  • Small team (~11 people): ~3 engineers, each with AI coding agent (events, baseline, QC); design uses AI for requirements → prototypes; ships fast with model-swappable mindset.

Q&A (app + session)

When are you moving to the US? (2 votes)

A: Targeting November (same year as conference).

Adopting AI without being outdated in months?

A: Architect workflows so you can swap models in/out — don’t bet the company on a single model API. Constant experimentation; internal adoption across eng / design / marketing.

How measure “imagination friction” on platform (staging / tours)?

A: Hard to quantify directly; proxy with funnel metrics — time on site / in experience, traffic → lead conversion, drop-off changes when AI visuals go live.

Upskill team to be AI overseers instead of only executing tasks?

A: Yes. Strong timelines, AI embedded in daily work — question becomes who steers whom: team vs AI. AI increasingly gives direction as well as execution help. Mentioned ecosystem where humans do last mile for AI agents (e.g. “task platforms” for AI — speaker’s reaction: early “Matrix” joke but serious trend).

One-liners

  • 800M users → AI access isn’t moat; execution + value creation is.
  • Imagination friction = visualisation gap → slow decisions.
  • Measure revenue / cost / cycle time, not tool adoption counts.
