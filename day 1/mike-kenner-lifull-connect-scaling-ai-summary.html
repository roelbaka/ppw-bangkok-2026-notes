From deck — AI: tool, not strategy

Strategic stance: deliberate second mover

The hype cycle
Only worth talking about if it moves the needle. Reality: consumer AI hasn’t changed outcomes for Lifull or peers — mostly PR + incremental automation. “AI is a tool, not the strategy. If it doesn’t move consumer metrics or margins, it’s just noise.”
Strategic patience
Watching & waiting · open-eyed, not first mover · agentic AI not mature. Status: not fully utilized yet — adopt as market proves value. Second mover to avoid bleeding-edge costs.
Moving the needle (four tests on slide)
(1) Remove leakage — auto-plug funnel revenue gaps
(2) Agent efficiency — slash time-per-transaction
(3) Reduce costs — lower CAC & overhead
(4) New capabilities — things users couldn’t do before (not just “better”)

Ticker/footer: conversation must be about outcomes — otherwise buzzwords.

Q: SEA brokers vary in tech — how do you help a low-tech partner perform better?

A: Distinction between portal model and transaction model. In core markets they’re not trying to make other brokers better at closing transactions; they want those partners to take leads if that’s the relationship. Strategic clarity: compete on transaction where they own the stack.

Q: Thailand IPO — still on the radar?

A: Need scale for a real outcome (~$100M-type bar mentioned for meaningful liquidity). Thailand business this year in the ~$15M range (per speech — verify). With growth, IPO could be serious in a few years. Market timing not ideal now; may be more of a SEA / regional listing story when the opportunity is clearer.

Q: Ideal parameters for acquisitions this year?

A: Not too small. Open to transaction or portal assets, even pre-revenue in some cases — case-by-case. Needs strong local operating partner in each market. Think across portal + transaction + finance angles broadly.

Q (floor): Is the core matching buyers/sellers and earning commission?

A: Yes. Everything else is to make that smoother and less friction; tech will keep shaving friction.

Q: What keeps you awake?

A: Occasionally existential (“what’s the point?”). Mainly: finding the exit / outcome after years of grind — Japanese parent could buy the business back; how to land a proper outcome for everyone.

Q: Next year — $50M goals, trajectory?

A: SEA team believes they’ll get very close organically with growth; Indonesia, Philippines, Cambodia transaction expansion; already entered Indonesia in part. Core SEA ~$35M on path toward ~$100M narrative. Focused regional assets easier to run and exit than messy global conglomerates.

Q: Why not sell Latin America?

A: Hard to sell a whole region; “no one’s empire building” like before. If sale doesn’t make sense, double down on SEA. Would sell at the right price if it did.

Q: Strip LatAm / freewheel / take cash?

A: Option value — not in a rush; could strip further later. Still see opportunity with right partners / strategic JV rather than force a fire sale.

Q: Why no LatAm (moderator)?

A: (Repeated) Had portal footprint vs transaction ambition but couldn’t find right model/partners/structure; won’t do for ego — may try again with better information.
