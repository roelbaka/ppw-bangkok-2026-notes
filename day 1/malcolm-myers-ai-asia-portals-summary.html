Will AI build or break Asia’s property portals?

Malcolm Myers — Founder & CEO, European Investment Ventures (EIV): cap raising (~$20M+), M&A, founder exits, private markets; works globally including Asia. Three-part talk: why classifieds struggle in Asia, how LLMs change search, how incumbents respond — and whether portal + brokerage is the path to scale.

2. Six criteria for classifieds to “work”

  1. Embedded — most market buyers use you.
  2. Traffic gap — typically ~3× vs #2.
  3. Turnover — liquid markets (not 30–40 year hold cycles).
  4. Material ASP — enough economics for agents to pay subscriptions.
  5. Professional agents — liquid corps that actually subscribe.
Singapore fits best; Malaysia complicated (two strong #1s); much of the region sits in the hard “middle” of vertical vs horizontal dominance.

3. Build or break?

Not a clean “break” AI search complements; high-involvement purchases ≠ low-involvement “six-pack” shopping.
Not enough to “build” alone Incumbent AI is incremental; structural issues (developers, informal market, agent productivity) remain.
Best shot Portal reach + data + brokerage monetization (e.g. Pinhome, HomeTeam narrative).
Social + AI Can strengthen marketing noise; portals still matter for depth, verification ambition, serious buyers.
Verdict: neither fully build nor break for now — Singapore leader unlikely disrupted soon; elsewhere model economics stay tough.

mogul.sg — FSBO + AI (example from Malcolm’s deck)

Singapore-focused example: AI-powered search and listing curation for the for-sale-by-owner (FSBO) path — blending discovery, data, and transaction-oriented UX.

  • Smart keyword search Smarter matching beyond basic filters.
  • Neighbourhood walkthrough Uses geospatial data to explore areas visually.
  • Past transaction data Grounds recommendations in comparable sales history.
  • Property agent (MAIA) AI-assisted agent-style guidance in the flow.
  • Buy with MRIA Transaction-forward step (e.g. “Buy now”–style journey on the platform).
  • Listing curation FSBO listings presented with a curated, productised experience.

Fits the talk’s theme: AI improving trust, discovery, and seller/buyer workflows — especially where incumbents and FSBO propositions compete on data depth and guided next steps, not just raw listings.

SocialFacebook groups (e.g. Philippines 300k+ members), marketplace, dev/agent pages, YouTube/TikTok ads, reviews — huge reach, low cost; weak verification, financing, pricing history, often lighter leads.
DevelopersPrimary sales huge; dev site traffic can rival portals; in some markets top few developers = half of sales; new-build share very high (e.g. China-style dynamics).
InformalLarge informal inventory dilutes how “big” portals look vs real demand; digitization is heavy lift.
Quality painTier-2 portals: weak listing quality, spam leads (“property never available”), undermines trust — room for something better.

AI search vs portals

AI+NL search, local language, schools/MRT; aggregates agency sites + data layers; synthesized top ~5 picks; fraud/title/dev checks emerging (e.g. Philippines-style depth).
Portal+See full market (e.g. thousands of listings), filters, years of behavioral engagement per listing.
GapAI good at comparing shortlisted options; easy to miss listings that didn’t tick every box. New builds often surfaced at project level vs unit-level depth on portals.

Core argument

Asia’s portals sit in a structurally harder box than Western vertical leaders: lower revenue per transaction, thinner traffic per home sold (worse if you add informal demand), and demand splintered across social and developers. LLMs improve discovery and synthesis but don’t replace the need to see the full market and trust the source.

Incumbents are shipping useful but incremental AI (verification, pricing, syndication, agent tools). The durable upside may be combining portal assets (brand, SEO traffic, listing + engagement data) with transaction-side monetization (brokerage economics), not ads alone.

Economics snapshot (from talk)

  • Western markets: much higher portal revenue per home sold vs most of Asia; extreme low examples (e.g. Indonesia ~$10) break pure subscription logic.
  • UK-style benchmark: order of magnitude more monthly uniques per home sold than many Asian markets — portals look weaker vs real housing churn + informal channels.

Short take

AI doesn’t magically fix Asia’s portal economics. It’s a tool for better discovery and workflows — but scale revenue may need portal + brokerage, while Singapore-style dominance stays the exception.

Q&A highlights

  • Valuation reset: Prove durable cash flows in an AI world; pay-per-lead / transaction-linked models; product-led leadership; innovation pace picking up in Europe.
  • Agentic search replacing portals? Unlikely for high-stakes property (vs low-involvement goods); analogous to travel hesitation.
  • Best LLM trial: Claude strong on multi-source integration — but watch overconfident recommendations.
  • Best UX pattern: Side-by-side classic filters + AI search (e.g. Homes.com-style), not replacement-only — respects 25 years of search habit.
Bottom line Build or break: neither for now. AI complements portals; structural Asia dynamics matter more than any single feature. Portal + brokerage is the plausible path to serious net revenue in harder markets.

Expanded summary

  • Global vs Asia: ~10 property portals above ~$100M revenue globally; Asia lacks that tier — PropertyGuru Singapore nearest, still sub-scale vs Western leaders.
  • Market shape: Winners sit at extremes (dominant vertical or dominant horizontal). Many Asian players sit in the middle — hardest competitive position.
  • Six pillars for classifieds success: embedding, 3× traffic vs #2, market liquidity, material prices, professional paying agents. Singapore fits; much of region does not.
  • Demand fragmentation: Social (groups, marketplace, video) and developer-led primary capture huge attention; informal sales undercount portal “share of reality.”
  • LLM property search: Cross-site aggregation, NL queries, risk/title/dev diligence emerging — strong for narrow shortlists; portals win on market breadth and behavioral data.
  • Startups: Interesting but not yet replicating portal + subscription economics at scale.
  • Incumbents: Verification, pricing intelligence, listing creation AI, syndication to Big Tech video — helpful, not transformative vs structural headwinds.
  • Deck example — mogul.sg: FSBO play in Singapore with AI search, geospatial neighbourhood exploration, past transactions, MAIA/MRIA-style assisted buying — illustrates AI + data + transaction UX on the sell-side path.
  • Conclusion: Singapore leader not obviously disrupted soon; social+AI amplifies marketing war; portal data + brokerage monetization cited as plausible winning combo (Pinhome, HomeTeam). Q&A: valuation recovery needs proof of cash generation and product credibility; hybrid search UI likely wins.
