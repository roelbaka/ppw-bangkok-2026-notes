Session

Fireside with Lukas Rose — ties to a prior “provocative” Madrid presentation on dominance, data, and intelligence. Below: voice memo summary (ASR cleaned).

What ProperBird does

  • Helps real estate players understand the agent landscape in a country: scans major portals, maps which agents are active where and how they show up across sites.
  • Product: Both raw data (for clients with large analytics teams) and increasingly intelligence / analysis — especially for smaller or challenger portals with less in-house analytical power.
  • Reproducibility: B2B PropTech clients expect analyses to be repeatable (run today → same logic tomorrow). Hard to slap non-deterministic AI on every query without breaking trust in reports.

AI inside ProperBird

  • Small, engineering-heavy team; Lukas’s example: ~2.5 years ago a few lines of code + AI → coin flip if useful; recently a large feature (~15k lines) largely AI-written in ~a week vs an estimated ~2 months manual.
  • Stack culture: Many models; Claude called out for precise coding; also OpenAI, Gemini, Cursor-style agents; everyone experiments — hard to stay on top.
  • Roadmap: Massive structured datasets → surface anomalies and insights with models (and eventually agent-style “worth looking at these 10 agents”); may change how decisions are prepared, not only made.

Raw data vs intelligence layer

Two customer tracks: Mature first-tier portals → likely keep consuming raw data for a long time. Smaller / challenger portals → need a partner for analytics on top of ingest.
  • Vision: unified ingest + NL-style questions for leadership — e.g. which segments are we strong in, duplication, where to focus.
  • Today’s strength: public listing data and agent activity patterns across portals. Growth edge: add traffic + monetization signals (hard to obtain) for richer, trust-bound partnerships.
  • Many large portals still run sales/ops with limited data rigour day-to-day — room to “get started” on data-driven ops.

Collection, structure, market picture

  • Hardest part is often not scraping but unstructured → structured and reliable dedupe/entity resolution.
  • Cold start (new country): Start collecting from portals; meaningful dataset in weeks. New portal in market: use aggregated existing market data. Even without a dominant portal, agency sites can be aggregated.
  • Listing leakage: Listings sometimes appear on agency sites weeks before the big portal — worth watching agencies as well as “primary” portals.
  • Many deals never hit the main portals (exclusive / off-market) — true picture may need many sources, not half a dozen. AI may eventually reduce bespoke scraper cost per site.
  • Tension: As portals push pricing, some agents keep inventory off platform — creates both noise and opportunity for intelligence vendors.

Trust, pricing, “iPhone 1 vs 17”

  • Portals are protective of internal data; early talks on combining external (ProperBird) + internal (traffic, pricing, audience) in a firewalled way — trust is the bottleneck.
  • Moderator: ultimate portal insight might be willingness-to-pay / optimal price per customer — would need listings + traffic + agent behaviour combined.
  • Lukas: current AI in portals (e.g. Home.com-style) like iPhone 1–2; lots still to come. ProperBird’s own maturity on the “full stack insight” journey: early innings, long road — MySpace-era analogy for how much can still change.

Q&A — questions & answers

1. Cold start / no historical data / new projects

A: Entering a new country is not a big cold-start problem: begin collecting from portals (and agencies), have a usable base in a few weeks. For a new portal entering a market, ProperBird can aggregate existing market data so they see where to focus. Alternative sources include agency sites when national portals are weak.

2. Who is the true market leader in France?

Not answered in the captured transcript (provocative audience question).

3. Do portals feed you data, or scrape? Industry standards?

A: They prefer to keep data collection under their own control (scraping / ingestion they run). There are standards for e.g. CRM → portal feeds — that’s not the main mode ProperBird relies on for their core product.

One-liners

  • Structure > scrape as the hard problem.
  • Big portals buy raw data; challengers need the intelligence layer.
  • Next unlock: traffic + monetization + trust.
