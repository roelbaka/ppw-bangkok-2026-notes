Below: cleaned summary from session transcript (ASR noise corrected where obvious).

What Snow Only does

  • Niche: Marketplace for ski / mountain property — fragmented market; buyers fear missing listings scattered across many sites.
  • Evolution: Started as discovery portal; now also education, coaching buyers, and helping transactions complete (high-value, cross-border).
  • Roles: Steve = product/tech; Mark = sales / buyer conversations — complementary (“nerd” + “sales guy”).

Business model: ads → success fee

  • Early: pay-to-list / subscription — agents didn’t know them; hard to sell even low monthly fees; had to prove value.
  • COVID (~10 months after launch): forced automation, fewer bodies, quantified outcomes, discipline.
  • Pivot: transaction / success fee — revenue only when deals close; “list for free” but if nothing completes, no income → had to own the ecosystem around the deal.
  • Cross-border pain: e.g. UK buyer vs French purchase — currencies, notaries, legal systems, mortgages, FX. Snow Only inserts value by coaching expectations from day one (budget realistic, what you can get) via content, site, video — but especially 1:1 conversation.
  • Long pipelines; deals drop (mortgage, notary, FX) — much outside their control; still they partner with experts (agents, FX, etc.) so the deal moves. Don’t chase highest commission on horizontals — what closes the deal matters.
  • Who pays: Developer deals → direct commission; agent-led → split with agent; ancillary services → commissions where aligned.
  • Scale without headcount: Agents in resort = hundreds of “sales feet on the ground” as partners, same goal: buyer gets the property.

Where AI helps — where it stops

AI “cuts off” after driving traffic, enriching product, and converting to inquiry — then Mark (human) owns the relationship: lifestyle, trust, hours-long chats on skiing and life change.
  • Automation mindset from day one; COVID accelerated it — “1% better every day.”
  • AI / tooling: Content, video, knowledge bases; image analysis → metadata, resort data, smarter search; APIs over property images.
  • French market analytics: Govt data, comps, percentile vs similar stock — work that would have been a major dev project; now ~20 minutes with AI — “would have been impossible” for a team their size without AI.
  • Build vs buy: Estimate internal build + maintenance vs SaaS; usually buy (e.g. partners like SearchSmart-type tools).
  • Human essential: Typical buyer ~€1M+ second home; wants peer conversation, lifestyle sell (ski culture, local colour). AI can’t replace that yet — “wait and see.”

Strategy & stress

  • Innovation: Conferences, podcasts, watch others, copy what works — not first movers, “nipping at ankles”; constant small experiments, kill or scale.
  • Who decides architecture: Steve + Mark from buyer/seller feedback — “always a new problem.”
  • Buyer’s agent: Rarely buys first listing seen — journey across resorts/budget; Snow Only positions as buyer-side guide.
  • SEO + AI: AI helped research SEO, trends, more relevant content, search- and user-friendly site — “game changer,” done in-house.
  • Non-English: Site is English-only; audience large enough for now.
  • Biggest worry: Factors outside control in international transactions — they’re early in funnel; chaos downstream — “go with the flow.”

Q&A — questions & answers

Blaine — How do buyers discover Snow Only?

Q: How do you market so buyers know the niche exists?

A: Little paid marketing in COVID period — invested in content and SEO. Strong organic + marketing to engaged subscribers. High-intent, low volume on purpose — not chasing masses of Thai (or random) traffic; want serious leads. Forms de-emphasise phone spam; they need to talk to qualify and tag urgency when passing leads to sellers/developers.

Tung — Remove human from 24/7 support & complex tax?

Q: AI faster for tax math / always-on support — take the human out?

A: Tax / specialist topics: Not their expertise — defer to experts. Post-inquiry “support”: Must stay human — Mark’s calls are relationship and lifestyle (hours), not transactional FAQ. Buyers are affluent peers who want that conversation; AI doesn’t deliver it yet.
