Talk summary — voice transcription (ASR cleaned)

Courtney Bugeja · PPW Bangkok · Final session of the day. Previous Pitch Club winner (~3 years ago).

Origin & positioning

Won Pitch Club last year; introduced idea: SellSmart = first platform to build an ecosystem around the digital twin to eliminate pain points of preparing for sale. Initially free tool for agents + consumers (efficient, transparent). Agent distribution still a core channel — but homeowners now actively seek this info themselves; behavior has moved upstream. Surprise: how early they prepare and how much money and decision-making happens before a listing exists.

Seller journey (reality)

Journey doesn’t start with the listing — starts up to 12 months earlier. Homeowners ask: Where does my property sit? What improvements? How much will it cost? Which agent will get the best result? They may invite one or two agents for a valuation; then prep begins — trades, advice, ideas. Listing = final step in a long process. Today: multiple agents, trades, photographers, copywriters, inspectors all visit and collect data independently — inefficient and time-consuming. Room poll: “How many people walked through before the listing went live?” — usually 8, 10, sometimes more. Digital twin eliminates that: one capture → everyone works from same dataset; trades quote remotely, agents submit proposals.

Digital twin ≠ marketing; ecosystem = opportunity

“Digital twin like a Porsche in the garage — without roads it goes nowhere.” Without an ecosystem, it stays a marketing asset; the real opportunity is the infrastructure around it. Past year focused on that. Customer journey: property captured once → homeowner can prepare and understand what it looks like. AI Home Maximization Report: AI analyzes the digital twin → recommendations on what to do, should do, cost estimates; benchmarked against local properties sold above market average = strategic blueprint. Homeowner moves from insight → action: quotes from trades (who assess the twin), model value of scenarios, engage agents with context (property + preparation strategy). Single ecosystem instead of chasing multiple agents and providers.

Spend & visibility

In most markets homeowners spend more preparing for sale than they pay in agent commission. Almost none of that prep happens on a platform today — that’s where the next major layer of the property ecosystem will emerge. Discovery: $5,000–$50,000+ (repairs, renovations, styling, maintenance) — and decisions are made months before the listing. Yet trades are contacted individually, advice fragmented; the largest portals don’t see this phase — they see the listing, not the months of preparation that shape valuation.

Agents: strategic advisers, not fee-extractors

Not “spray and pray” to agents. Position agents as strategic advisers, not transaction processors. Through the platform they submit structured proposals: target market, recommended improvements/repairs, go-to-market and marketing strategy, why they’re the best. When sellers compare, “the difference becomes clear — it separates charisma from strategy.” Sentiment from agents has been very good.

New data layer

Ecosystem unlocks data that doesn’t exist on portals or trade directories. Because the process happens on-platform: trade pricing across property types, renovation decisions, order homeowners prioritize improvements, agent strategies and how they influence other decisions, property condition, improvement cycles, when homeowners are preparing to enter the market. When digital twin is the starting point, it becomes infrastructure, not marketing. Emerging patterns: how cohorts behave over time, what they prioritize, what improvements influence sale outcome — new layer of property intelligence.

After the sale

Homeowners who used the platform in the presale phase want to keep using it — in their new home. They request another digital twin; purpose shifts to managing improvements, upgrades, renovations → infrastructure becomes more like a home operating system.

Implication for portals

Portals today capture the listing moment. The most important decisions happen months earlier. Digital twins create the infrastructure; the real opportunity is what happens around it. If portals could see the preparation stage they’d gain extraordinary insight into seller intent, property improvements, market readiness. “This data changes everything.”

Revenue (Australia example)

Trade / retail economy ~$5B (excl. conveyancing, renovations, utilities). Post-sale trade & improvement ~$24B per year. Plus agent-intro revenue; plus data & insights (e.g. insurers). Stacked on an existing listing business = not just incremental — a multiplier on the entire platform.

Closing

Digital twins will become standard infrastructure. But long-term value isn’t in the infrastructure — it’s in platforms that capture seller intent earliest. That moment is months before the listing reaches the portal. “The companies that control that signal control the next generation of property transactions.”

Audience Q&A — Whova + answers (transcription)

Session: Questions for The New Point of Co… (SellSmart).

Q (Tung): Is your solution like an AI scorecard for potential sellers before they list?

A: Moderator noted there are many ways SellSmart engages with sellers to determine readiness to come to market, and the same is applied to supply-side businesses on the platform; asked if the question could be rephrased more directly. No full answer from Courtney to the “scorecard” framing.

Q (Harvey): If I spend 10k on staging and improving my property before listing, how much more can I sell it for?

A: “How long is a piece of string” — lots of factors, every market different. If the home is vacant, styling generally helps sell faster and for more (emotional connection for buyer). Without knowing the market it’s hard to give a number. Staging is “quite important” — e.g. the room example in the talk would “absolutely decimate” value if left as-is; staging that room would “release a lot of value.” Bottom line: we don’t give a single number, but if you’re selling, definitely stage your house.

Q: Does it have to be your own digital twin you analyse, or can you ingest [another]?

A: “We use Matterport at the moment — we find them the best solution in the market. We are analyzing the Matterport digital twin.” So they can ingest Matterport twins.

Q (Harvey): Repositioning agents as strategic advisor vs “fee-extractor” — are agents enjoying this evolution or stuck in the mud?

A: Some agents rise, some keep doing the same. On the platform, fee is the last thing we look at — if an agent demonstrates they understand your market, have the right strategy, will get the best price, their fee is irrelevant. “When you have three [proposals] in front of you you can see the stock standard that came through AI, the best guy in market with most knowledge, and the guy in the middle” — the tool helps bridge the gap. Evolution from “fee extractor” to strategic advisor has to happen across the industry (portals, agents, developers) — be more knowledgeable, otherwise AI does the “knowledge” and sometimes the answers are wrong (anecdote: son put diesel in petrol car because ChatGPT told him to). Best agents have strong market knowledge; on-screen example showed one proposal very specific on target buyer market vs one generalist “targeting everyone” — “you see the differences when you read the proposals.”

Q (Jonathan): How many digital twins have you created so far?

A: “A few thousand now. Business has grown … from where we were a year ago, good year.”

Q (Harvey, rephrased): What uplift in selling prices have you seen when sellers improve their property?

A: “Massive improvements. Different markets, different things — sometimes spending [e.g. $2.5k], sometimes spending $10k might give you $80k more. It’s about being strategic about improvements and repairs to position it in the best way and not over-capitalizing. On our website there’s a section with before/after case studies — you see the uplift they achieved.”

Moderator (Simon) — funding & roadmap

Q: Have you raised money?

A: Yes; “liked the clothes in my round”; now up for another round, looking forward to the next 12 months.

Q: How much did you raise?

A: “I ended up raising about $1M last year. I still got about 12 months runway because revenues kept growing and burn reduced — capital efficiency kept expanding. 12 months runway in the bank.”

Q: How do you plan to deploy the capital?

A: Investment in technology, marketing / brand, and people. Two focus points: (1) Nailing the top of funnel — marketing and strategic partners. (2) What keeps him up at night: how we use AI so the funnel doesn’t leak — e.g. if we catch someone 9–12 months out from a decision, how do systems nurture them until they’re ready without the team having to do it?

Q: Is that your biggest headache?

A: “It feels like my biggest headache right now. Yeah.”
