Conference learnings — closing panel (digest)

Malcolm Myers (eiv) · Jonathan Turpin (AIM) · Simon Baker (Online Marketplaces). Full ASR-cleaned notes: panel-conference-learnings

  • People: Surround the business with AI power users — productivity gap is real; culture must change, not only tools (newspaper-era parallel).
  • Product: Data in portal hands beats “LLM alone” for search quality — moat is capture + structure, not chat veneer.
  • Strategy: Tension between exposing listings/content for LLM discovery vs protecting proprietary inventory — design layers, not one rule.
  • Distribution: Google / Facebook history rhymes; social discovery still under-counted; trust + brand matter for bounce-back.
  • UX: Voice / conversational search may be a real paradigm shift; Gen Z skepticism of “AI slop” on high-stakes buys.
  • Org: Legacy stack + fast experiment teams — weekend shipping vs Monday governance.
  • Macro: AI’s society / employment impact may dwarf property-only angst; vertical complexity keeps transaction helpers valuable.

Cross-cutting themes (merged)

1 · AI search: data beats theatre

One line: Referral from AI to portals is still tiny; organic hasn’t collapsed overnight — but behaviour (zero-click, AI Mode, fewer searches per user) compounds. The work is three layers (traditional SEO → answer-ready → citation-worthy) and being in the answer, not only chasing clicks.

Where to go deeper: Van der Heijden · Keith · Panel (traditional portals)

2 · The middle mile: data, matching, explainability

One line: Integrating AI into search is mostly data quality + structure + user/place/advertiser signals; the chat layer is the easy part. Recommendations must be trustworthy and explainable; commercial boosts and merit-based matching can coexist in one engine.

Where to go deeper: Dar (SearchSmartly)

3 · Portals, agents & platform power

One line: AI reframes the portal–agent relationship and accelerates brokerage-as-marketplace models — who owns workflow, data, and the customer.

Where to go deeper: Liu (Soho.ai) · Baker & Künzi

4 · New build & specialised marketplaces

One line: AI and product design reshape new-build discovery, inventory, and marketplace mechanics — not only resale portals.

Where to go deeper: Bird & Baker

5 · Search widens into due diligence

One line: The journey extends beyond find-a-listing into verification, risk, and buyer confidence — product opportunity adjacent to core search.

Where to go deeper: Rogers (Beforeyoubuy)

6 · Marketing & distribution off-portal

One line: Off-site marketing and brand spend — what actually moves the needle vs noise.

Where to go deeper: Sertis

7 · Building the AI-native organisation

One line: Culture, hiring, governance, and operating model — not only model choice and demos.

Where to go deeper: Panel (AI-native business)

8 · Conference learnings — closing panel

One line: After ~48h on AI and transactions, the panel converges on talent/culture, data moats, distribution history (Google, social), and honest UX (voice, anti-slop) — without pretending property is isolated from macro AI shock.

Pull-through (Malcolm): AI-native operators and weekend shipping vs legacy core; newspaper transition mindset.

Pull-through (Turpin): Sessions proved portals’ data acquisition story — LLMs don’t replace that asset.

Pull-through (Simon): Portal–Google “cold sweat” replay; Facebook’s hidden share; Homes.com-style conversational try changed his view on voice; generational trust.
